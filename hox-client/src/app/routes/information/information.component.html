General Information

Welcome to the World Cup 2018 trading application!

Make sure to join the Whatsapp chat group.

The trading is modelled as financial forwards, where each participating team is represented by a tradable instrument. You can both buy and sell a team depending on your belief in the teams performance in the tournament. The teams are traded on an open market, where the price is decided by what other traders are willing to buy and sell the teams for.

At the end of the tournament (or when a team is eleminated), each trade is settled according to a price decided on how the team performs in the tournament. I.e. if you buy a team at a price decided by the market, you will then sell at a settlement price decided by the teams placement. In the same way, if you instead sell a team on the market, at settlement you will buy back at the settlement price.

The Settlement prices are calculated as follows:

  • 1:st 30
  • 2:nd 25
  • 3:rd 20
  • 4:th 15
  • Quarter-finals 10
  • Round of 16 5
  • Group Stage 0

E.g. in World Cup 2014 England was knocked out in the Group Stage, and thus got 0 points. Costa Rica did an impressive effort and got all the way to the quarter-finals and was therefore awarded 10 points. And the winner, Germany, got the highest score of 30 points!

So, in short, if you belive that a team will perform good in the tournament, you should aim to buy that team for a price lower than what you think think their settlement price will be.
If you on the other hand believe a team will perform badly, you should sell that team to a price higher that what you think they will settle at in the end.

Calculating the result is fairly simple. You buy a quantity q at a price pB with a total value of vB. And you sell the same quantity q at a price pS with a total value of vS. The profit/loss (PnL) is calculated as the difference between the total sell value and the total buy value as follows:

PnL = vS - vB = q * (pS - pB)

The calculation is the same wheter you buy or sell in the open market, it's just a matter in which order the transactions take place. If you take a long position (i.e. buy in the open market), pB is the price that you paid, and pS will be the settlement price. Conversely, if you take a short position (i.e. sell in the open market), pS is the price that you sold for, and pB will be the settlement price.

Example: Long position

You are convinced that Germany will win this year again. From the settlement table above you can see that if they win, they will settle at 30 SEK. The market on the other hand don't believe as strongly in Germany, and the price to buy is therefore only 20 SEK. A bargin you think, and therefore buy a quantity of 10.

vB = q * pB = 10 * 20 = 200 SEK

In this example Germany goes to final, but loses (i.e. end up in 2:nd place). The settlement will thus be done at 25 SEK per contract.

vS = q * pS = 10 * 25 = 250 SEK =>
PnL = vS - vB = q * (pS - pB) = 10 * (25 - 20) = 50 SEK.

So, even though Germany didn't score gold, the settlement price was still higher than the price you paid in the open market, and therefore you made a profit of 50 SEK.

OBSERVE!
If Germany would instead end up in 4:th place (settlement price of 15 SEK) you would instead lose money on the bet.

PnL = q * (pS - pB) = 10 * (15 - 20) = -50 SEK.
Example: Short position

What if you believe that Germany won't be able to perform as good as last time, and at best reach the Quarter-finals? Then you should take a short position (i.e. sell in the open market) at a price higher than 10 SEK. The market offers to buy at 14 SEK, so you proceed to buy 20 contracts.

vS = q * pS = 20 * 14 = 280 SEK

Your prediction becomes true. Germany is kicked out in the quarter finals, and is therefore settled at 10 SEK.

vB = q * pB = 20 * 10 = 200 SEK =>
PnL = vS - vB = q * (pS - pB) = 20 * (14 - 10) = 80 SEK.

Betting on a team to perform badly can be just as fruitful, and you made a profit of 80 SEK.

Trading

On the Overview page you'll find a list of all the teams with the current price to Buy or Sell. Click the blue Buy button, or the red Sell button to go to the order entry page. Fill out the form and press Submit to send the order to the market. The orders that you create will be visible on the Orders page.

If there's an order on the opposite side with the same (or more favourable) price, the orders will be matched and you will get a trade which can be seen on the Trades page. If the order is not matched directly, you can either wait for it to be matched when someone enters a matching order. Or you can withdraw the order by clicking the Withdraw button next to the order.

There's also a possibility to click the team name on the overview page, to get to the details page. On the details page you can see a complete view of all the Buy and Sell levels on the market. You can also see a chart of how the team has been traded historically.

Furthermore the overview also contains a Statistics table where you can see how each team succeeded in the last World Cup (2014), and an average of the team's results for the last 5 World Cups. Click the team name (with a flag in front) to see a more detailed view.

Settlement

Settlement processing is automated, and will be triggered as soon as a settlement price can be decided (i.e. when a team's final placement in the tournament is known). This means that settlements can be done throughout the tournament as soon as a team gets kicked out. When a settlement instruction has been created it will appear on the Settlements page.

Since trading is settled peer-to-peer, you will get one settlement instruction per counterparty. If you are the payer, you will get a QR code for Swishing the settlement amount to you counterparty. Don't forget to acknowledge when you have swished the settlement amount, and when you have recieved the settlement amount.

OBSERVE!
For Swish settlement to work, make sure that you have filled out a Swish-connected phone number on your User profile page. Click the User silhouette in the menu bar to view and edit your user.

FAQ
Why do the derivative names end with Jul 18?

The instruments are automatically named according to the commodity derivatives convention of using the underlying name plus the month and year of expiry. Since World Cup ends in July 2018, the name becomes e.g. Germany Jul 18.

Why is the expiry date July 16:th, when the final is on the 15:th?

The applocation has a builtin restriction that only allows trading up until the day before settlement. But in order to keep betting alive during the final the expiry has been set to the day after. So! When the final is over, make sure that you don't have any active orders left in the market, or you may be hit badly.
Actually this is relevant throughout the whole tournament. If e.g. England is kicked out in the group stage, we will then know that their settlement price will be 0. But since all derivatives expire on July 16:th, it will technically be possible to continue trading in England. You should therefore make sure that you don't have any outstanding orders in teams that has been kicked out of the tournament, or you may be hit badly.

How do I invite someone to the game?

Navigate to the User profile page. Under Invites click the New invite button to generate a new invite code.
Click the copy icon next to the code to copy a complete invite link to the clipboard. The invite is valid for one registration, and when it has been used you will instead see the name of the person that used the invite.
You can create as many invites as you like. But be aware of that it is logged who you invite; so do feel responsible for whom you invite.
